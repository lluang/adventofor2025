üìê The extensibility test
When new [[requirements]] appear, run them through this mental checklist:

Does this change the [[decisions]]? New actions to take, or just refinements of existing ones?
Does this change the [[objective]]? A new goal, or an additional consideration within the same goal?
Does this change the [[constraints]]? New rules, or variations of existing limits?
Does this change the [[data requirements]]? New inputs, or just more granular versions of what we have?
Does this change the structure? New entities, or subdivisions of existing sets?
If the new requirements fit into the same five building blocks, your approach can handle them. You're adding complexity, not changing direction.

If they require new building blocks entirely, you might be looking at a different problem.



üéØ The response
When stakeholders ask "can your approach handle X?", the answer is rarely a flat yes or no.

The professional response is: "Here's what we'd need to get it..."

This does three things:

Confirms your approach is flexible ([[building trust]])
Identifies the data/information gaps ([[setting expectations]])
Moves the conversation forward (keeping momentum)
Never promise before you understand.

But never dismiss before you explore.
